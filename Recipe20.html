Laddu

Ingredients

    • 1 kg Besan
    • Water (as per need)
    • Oil (For frying)
  • For the Syrup:
    • 1 Â½ kg Sugar
    • 700 ml Water
  • Dry Fruits:
    • 70 gms Cashew
    • 30 gms Raisins
    • 2 pinches Wet camphor (Edible)
    • 1 tsp Cardamom (Ilaichi) Powder

Recipe Steps

  1. Run the Besan through a fine sieve. Prepare a batter with water, making sure that there are no lumps.
  2. Melt the sugar and prepare syrup of one-string consistency.
  3. While the sugar is boiling, take away any impurities that may float to the top. As soon as the syrup reaches a one-string stage, take the syrup off the flame.
  4. Heat the oil. Hold the perforated ladle close to the oil and drop the batter into pearl-like Boondis into the oil through the ladle. Follow the tips about how to drop the Boondi into the oil.
  5. Fry till golden brown and take out the Boondis. Do not drop too much Boondi into the Kadhai at one time.
  6. As soon as the Boondi is taken out, put it into the syrup. Dunk it into the syrup nicely with the help of a large spoon. Keep adding the rest of the Boondi into the syrup. Mix well.
  7. Let the Boondi cool down in the syrup.
  8. After two hours, mix Cardamom Powder, Camphor, and dry fruit (Cashew and Raisins) into the laddoo mixture. Mix well.
  9. Rub ghee on the palms and make laddoos, squeezing out the Boondis tightly.
  10. After the laddoos are made, leave them undisturbed for an hour. The laddoos will firm up.
  11. This laddoo can remain fresh and juicy even after a week.
