Fish Curry

Ingredients

  • To Marinate Fish
    • 1 tbsp Mirchi Powder
    • 1/2 tsp Pepper Powder
    • Water - a little
    • Salt
    • 1/2 kilo Fish Pieces
  • For Gravy
    • 4 tbsp Oil
    • 1/2 tsp Pepper
    • 1/2 tsp Fennel Seeds
    • 1/2 tsp Cumin Seeds
    • 10 Garlic
    • 5 Red Chillies
    • 2 Onions (finely chopped)
    • 1/4 tsp Turmeric
    • 2 Sprigs Curry leaves
    • 1/4 cup Raw Coconut
    • 2 tbsp Coriander Powder
    • 2.5 tbsp Mirchi Powder
    • 3 Tomatoes (finely chopped)
    • Tamarind – Lemon sized
  • For the Stew
    • 4 tbsp Oil
    • 1 tsp Mustard Seeds
    • 1/4 tsp Fenugreek Seeds
    • 6 Garlic
    • 2 Sprigs Curry Leaves
    • 2 Green Chillies Sliced
    • 10 Small Onions
    • Rock Salt
    • 300 ml Water
    • Green Coriander – Small bunch

Recipe Steps

  1. On a plate, add some Mirchi Powder, Salt, Pepper Powder, and some Water to make a paste. Coat the Fish pieces with the paste and let it rest.
  2. Heat Oil in a pan and add Pepper, Cumin Seeds, Fennel Seeds, Red Chillies, and Garlic. Fry well.
  3. Add the chopped Onions, Curry Leaves, and Turmeric. Cover and cook until the Onions soften.
  4. In the Onions, add the Raw Coconut pieces, Coriander Powder, Mirchi Powder, Tomato pieces, and Green Coriander. Let it cook on medium flame until the Oil comes to the surface.
  5. Once the tomatoes are cooked, add them to a mixie jar along with Tamarind juice and make a soft paste.
  6. Heat Oil in a pan and add Mustard Seeds and Fenugreek Seeds. Let the Mustard Seeds splutter.
  7. Add Fennel Seeds, Cumin, Curry Leaves, Garlic, Green Chillies, and the Sambar Onions. Fry until golden.
  8. Take the Tomatoes made into a paste with the Tamarind Water and add Rock Salt. Cook on a low flame until it changes colour and the Oil separates. This will give you thick, tasty Gravy.
  9. Add 300 ml Water and cook the Gravy on a medium flame for 15 minutes until Oil comes to the surface.
  10. Add the Fish pieces and Fish Eggs (if you have them), cover it, and cook on a low flame until Oil separates.
  11. Add Green Coriander along with stalks and Curry Leaves along with stalks. Switch off the stove and leave it untouched for an hour.
  12. Serve the Stew with hot Rice, Chapathi, Dosa, or Idly.
