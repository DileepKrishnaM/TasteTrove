Poornam

Ingredients

  • For top layer
    • 1/2 cup Urad Dal (Soaked for 4 hours)
    • 1 cup Rice flour
    • Salt - pinch
    • Water - Sufficient
  • For Green gram filling
    • 1 cup Moongdal (Soaked for 4 hours)
    • 1 cup Grated Raw Coconut
    • 1 1/4 cup Grated Jaggery
    • 1/2 tsp Cardamom powder
    • 1 tsp Ghee
    • 1 tbsp Water
    • Oil - For frying the sweet balls (Poornalu)

Recipe Steps

  1. Add a little salt to the rice flour, make a soft dough and keep it aside for an hour.
  2. Grind the Urad Dal adding water into a smooth batter.
  3. Add both the rice flour dough and the Urad Dal batter, adding the required water, and keep it aside (Follow the tips for getting proper consistency).
  4. Grind the soaked Moong Dal like the batter we make for Idli, smooth as we make for Dosas.
  5. Steam them, placing small portions into the Idli plate and cook for 5 minutes on high flame and 4 minutes on low flame.
  6. Make a smooth rava of the steamed Idlis without lumps (It becomes a proper rava once it is cooled down).
  7. Heat some ghee in the pan and fry grated coconut for one minute.
  8. Pour the jaggery water in the pan and let it boil till you see one bubble.
  9. Add Moong Dal rava to this jaggery mixture and stir it properly without lumps. Allow the mixture to become thick.
  10. After it cools down, make lemon-sized balls and keep them aside.
  11. Dip these balls in the rice flour batter and remove the excess batter so that a thin layer remains.
  12. Fry them in hot oil on medium flame until they turn golden brown and crispy.
  13. They remain fresh and crispy even after a few hours.
